#1 Maryland Mountain 🏆 Tier A
🚗 ~45 min / 28 mi 📍 Black Hawk, CO ⬆️ ~1,000 ft descent
✅ FREE · No permit needed Open any day
▼

The crown jewel of free Front Range eMTB. A purpose-built, bike-only downhill park with machine-built berms, tabletops, and rhythm sections — the same format as a lift-accessed bike park, but free. Motor up Easy Money, then lap Fast Money and Hard Money all afternoon.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trail type: Bike-only, directional downhill descents. No hikers on descent trails.

Flow: Dedicated climb trail to summit → pick your descent → ride back to trailhead → repeat. Pure lap riding system.

Trailhead: Hidden Treasure Trailhead — 8060 Black Hawk Blvd, Black Hawk, CO 80422. Free parking lot. Well-signed.

Hours: Open daily, no seasonal bike restrictions. Best April–October.

💚 FREE — No permit, no day pass, no even-day rule. Just show up, park, and ride. One of the only fully free purpose-built downhill bike parks on the Front Range.

#2 Apex Park ⚡ Tier B
🚗 ~30 min / 22 mi 📍 Golden, CO ⭐ Closest Tier B
✅ FREE 📅 Even Days Only (weekends)
▼

Enchanted Forest is fast and loose — high-speed winding descent that rewards momentum. Less manicured than a bike park but the spirit is the same: climb up, send it down. Carries some loose chunk you'll fly through at speed. Solid Tier B that's close to Tier A on a good day.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trail type: Directional singletrack on descent trails (even days). Shared climbing trails any day.

Flow: Climb via Heritage Trail or Kinney Run → descend via Enchanted Forest or Magic Mountain combo. True top-to-bottom laps on even days.

Trailhead: 3052 Heritage Rd, Golden, CO 80401. Arrive before 9am on even-day weekends — lot fills fast.

Access note: Grubstake Trail access from water tank road permanently closed Summer 2025 — use Kinney Run as alternate.

📅 EVEN-DAY RULE (weekends only): Bikes only on even-numbered calendar dates (2nd, 4th, 6th…). Odd days = hikers & equestrians only. Weekdays are shared-use any day. Check the date before you drive out. Today's status shown in the header above.

#3 Virginia Canyon Mountain Park 🏆 Tier A UPDATED 2026
🚗 ~45 min / 37 mi 📍 Idaho Springs, CO ⬆️ 1,300 ft / 4 mi pedal climb
✅ FREE to pedal 💛 Gondola fee (optional) Open any day
▼

This place has completely leveled up. What was a solid pedal-up bike system is now one of the most exciting eMTB destinations on the Front Range — 16 miles of trail open, most descents are bike-only directional, and the Mighty Argo Cable Car (opened May 2026) gives you lift-access laps for a fee. Pedaling up is still free and totally doable on the Copperhead. Descents include big berms, rock features, jumps, and wooden features. Growing toward 28 miles total. Intermediate to expert — this is not a beginner hill.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Parking: No dedicated trailhead lot — park on Colorado Blvd in Idaho Springs or in designated town lots. Use parkidahosprings.com to plan. Lot fills early on weekends.

Getting to the trailhead: From parking, ride the Clear Creek Greenway through town to the trail entrance. Connect to Riverside Drive via the pedestrian bridge at CRC Park or 23rd Ave, then ride up the diagonal ramp past the Mighty Argo base to the upper bench where trails begin.

Trail type: 16+ miles of trails — mix of bike-only directional descents, bike uphill-only climbs, and hiker-only trails. Descent trails are fully separated from hikers.

Option 1 — Pedal up (FREE): Take Rosa Gulch Trail from the upper bench. Bikes go uphill only on this trail — hikers also use it but traffic is separated by direction. Steady singletrack climb, 4 miles / 1,300 ft to the top at Miners Point (8,800 ft). Continuous but gentle zig-zag grade — very manageable on the Copperhead in Eco. Reach the Hub at ~2 miles (halfway, great views of Clear Creek) — can access Hopper and Gauntlet Gold descents from here. Continue another 2 miles to the summit to access Drop Shaft and the full upper trail system.
Option 2 — Mighty Argo Cable Car (fee required): North America's first gondola bike cabins. 1.2-mile ride, gains 1,300 ft in ~10 minutes. Drops you at Miners Point (8,800 ft) — same summit as the pedal climb, direct access to all descent trails. Onsite parking at the Argo Mill is $9.95 for 4 hours. Book tickets at mightyargo.com. Bike-specific gondola cabins — roll your bike in vertically. Intermediate/advanced riders only — the gondola itself warns "No Beginners." Use pedal climb for first lap to preview trails, then use gondola for subsequent laps if desired.

#4 Centennial Cone Park ⚡ Tier B
🚗 ~50 min / 34 mi 📍 Golden / Idaho Springs, CO 📅 Even days (weekends)
✅ FREE 📅 Even Days Only (weekends) ⚠️ Closed Dec 1–Jan 31
▼

Travois Trail is the reason to come here — flowing singletrack threading through pine groves above Clear Creek Canyon. Smooth, fast, good sightlines — not a rock garden. The eMTB motor turns the long perimeter climb into something genuinely fun, leaving full legs for the descent.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trail type: Singletrack loop system. On even days, descent trails are directional bike-only. Shared climbing trails any day weekdays.

Flow: Park at Mayhem Gulch lot → climb Mayhem Gulch Trail → follow Centennial Cone Trail counterclockwise gaining elevation → descend Travois Trail → return to trailhead. Good lap structure.

Trailhead: Two trailheads on Clear Creek Canyon Rd west of Golden — Mayhem Gulch lot is the main one.

Land manager: Jefferson County Open Space. Class 1 eMTBs confirmed (7 designated eMTB trails).

📅 EVEN-DAY RULE (weekends): Bikes only on even-numbered dates. Odd days = hikers & equestrians only. Weekdays open any day. Also closed Dec 1 – Jan 31. Elk Range Trail additionally closed Feb 1 – June 15 for elk calving.
🗺️ All Trails

Centennial Cone Trail (lower climb) Climb Blue Multi-use Follow counterclockwise to gain elevation through open meadow and pine forest.
★ Travois Trail Descent Blue Bike Only (even) The payoff. Flowing singletrack through pine groves above Clear Creek Canyon. Smooth, fast, good sightlines. The trail you came for.

#5 White Ranch Park ⚡ Tier B NEW
🚗 ~35 min / 23 mi 📍 Golden, CO ⬆️ ~1,740 ft climb
✅ FREE · No permit Open any day
▼

Largest park in the Jeffco system — 21+ miles of multi-use trails over 4,000 acres. Belcher Hill is a legendary grind (brutal on a regular MTB, manageable on eMTB) and the Mustang Trail descent is what everyone comes for: fast, technical, bermy corners, drops, and forested singletrack. The motor completely transforms this place — what used to be a suffer-fest is now a genuine all-day lap session.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trail type: Singletrack — multi-use throughout. Longhorn Trail has a 0.9-mile bike-only, one-way section. No even-day rule.

Flow: Two options: (1) Lower East Trailhead on W 56th Ave — climb Belcher Hill (~1,740 ft gain) to the bench, then descend Mustang or Rawhide. (2) Upper West Trailhead on Belcher Hill Rd — skip the big climb and access upper trails directly.

Trailhead: East Trailhead: W 56th Ave, Golden CO (off Hwy 93). West Trailhead: Belcher Hill Rd (upper access, skip the main climb). Note: Mustang/Sawmill area has trail improvements Jun–Jul 2026.

Land manager: Jefferson County Open Space. eMTBs confirmed on natural-surface trails.

💚 FREE — No permit, no day pass, no even-day rule. Open any day year-round. The big climb is the only barrier — and your motor handles that.

#6 Staunton State Park ⚡ Tier B NEW
🚗 ~55 min / 40 mi 📍 Conifer, CO ⬆️ 9,500 ft elevation
💛 $10 Day Pass Open any day
▼

Colorado's newest state park (opened 2013) — granite cliffs, aspen groves, high meadows, and 38 MTB-designated trails. eMTBs allowed on all state park trails (not on USFS land portions like Rusty Buckle or North Elk). Great flow on Bear Paw and Mason Creek sections. Feels more remote and less crowded than Jeffco parks. Good for days when Apex/Centennial are closed (odd days).

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trail type: Singletrack loop system — mixed multi-use and bike-friendly. No directional restrictions or even-day rules.

Flow: Loop-based system — no single dedicated climb. Start from Parking Area #1 (southernmost), follow Staunton Ranch Trail east. Standard route: Staunton Ranch Trail → Marmot Passage → Border Line → Mason Creek → Bear Paw → back. 3–4 hour adventure day.

Trailhead: 92 Crawford Gulch Rd, Golden, CO 80403. Multiple parking areas — Lot #1 is main. $10 day pass pay station at entry.

eMTB note: eMTBs NOT allowed on Rusty Buckle or North Elk Creek trails — those sections cross USFS (National Forest) land. All other trails: eMTBs confirmed.

Hours: 5am–10pm daily.

💛 $10/vehicle day pass — required. Pay at the entry gate or kiosk. Colorado State Parks Annual Pass ($80) covers entry — pays for itself in ~8 visits. No reservation needed for day use. Wildlife closures Mar–Jul may affect some trails — check cpw.state.co.us before riding.

#7 Marshall Mesa / Doudy Draw 🌄 Tier C
🚗 ~20 min / 14 mi 📍 Superior / Boulder, CO ⭐ Closest to Home
✅ FREE · No permit Open any day 5am–11pm
▼

Your closest option — 20 minutes off Hwy 93. XC flow rather than bike-park berms, but Springbrook Loop has genuine swoopy fun on the descent with incredible Flatirons views. Best for after-work laps, testing new gear, or days when you don't want to drive far.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trail type: Multi-use singletrack and gravel. No directional or even-day restrictions. Out-and-back or loop structure.

Flow: Park at Doudy Draw Trailhead → climb Doudy Draw Trail (smooth, non-technical, ~2 mi) → connect to Springbrook Loop via Community Ditch → descend Springbrook for the views and flow → return.

Trailhead: Doudy Draw Trailhead — 2601 Eldorado Springs Dr, Superior, CO 80027. Small lot — arrive before 9am on weekends.

eMTB warning: eMTBs NOT allowed on Goshawk Ridge or any trail without the eBike symbol. Check signage at junctions.

Seasonal note: May close during muddy spring conditions — check bouldercolorado.gov before going.

💚 FREE — No permit, no day pass. City of Boulder Open Space. 10 designated eMTB trails. Hours: 5am–11pm daily.

#8 Floyd Hill Open Space ⚡ Tier B UPDATED
🚗 ~40 min / 30 mi 📍 Floyd Hill / Idaho Springs, CO ⬆️ Trailhead starts at ~8,000 ft
✅ FREE · No permit Open any day
▼

The key detail most people miss: the trailhead starts at nearly 8,000 ft, so the eMTB climb is short and easy compared to any other park on this list. The Sluice is the reason to come — the Front Range's first purpose-built bike-only directional downhill trail, with jumps, berms, drops, step-ups, step-downs, rock lines, and 700 ft of descent in 1.2 miles. Double black with a rideable single black main line. Built by FlowRide Concepts, same crew that built Maryland Mountain and Rutabaga. Solid odd-day alternative when Apex and Centennial Cone are hiker-only.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trailhead: Floyd Hill Open Space parking lot on top of Floyd Hill — take I-70 Exit 248 (Floyd Hill/Beaver Brook), head west on US-40. Parking lot is on the west side of the large brown building at the top. Ample parking.

Trail type: Mix — climb loops are multi-use shared with hikers going up. The Sluice descent is bike-only directional. Hikers use the Skip Trail to descend (separate from The Sluice).

Flow: Park at summit-level trailhead (~8,000 ft) → short climb on Portal + Floyd's Loop + Canyon View Loop (multi-use, shared going up, clockwise recommended) → drop into The Sluice (bike-only, downhill only) → short non-technical 200 ft re-climb back to the lot → repeat laps. The eMTB makes the re-climb trivial. Described as "how many laps can you do in a day?"

Land manager: Clear Creek County Open Space. Class 1 eMTBs confirmed.

💚 FREE — No permit, no day pass, no even-day rule. Open any day. Less crowded than most Jeffco parks. Good summer option — high elevation keeps it cooler.

#9 Golden Gate Canyon State Park 🌄 Tier C
🚗 ~50 min / 35 mi 📍 Gilpin / Jefferson County 🏔️ 12,000 acres
💛 $10 Day Pass Open any day 5am–10pm
▼

Less about pure flow, more about a big beautiful day out. 12,000 acres, aspen groves, high meadows, sweeping descents. Best when you want an adventure rather than laps. The eMTB transforms what would be an exhausting XC epic into an enjoyable all-day mission. Save this for full adventure days.

🚦 Access & How It Works

Trail type: Multi-use singletrack and doubletrack loop system — no directional or even-day rules. 38 of 44 trails open to MTB.

Flow: Loop-based — climbing and descending are woven throughout. Best approach: start at Bootleg Bottom Trailhead and follow Mule Deer Loop clockwise (~17km). Not ideal for pure lap-riding — plan for a 3–4 hour adventure day.

Trailhead: Main park entrance: 92 Crawford Gulch Rd, Golden, CO 80403. Multiple trailheads throughout — Bootleg Bottom for Mule Deer Loop. $10 vehicle day pass at entry gate.

Hours: 5am–10pm daily. Wildlife closures Mar–Jul on some trails — check cpw.state.co.us.

💛 $10/vehicle day pass — required. Pay at entry gate. Colorado State Parks Annual Pass ($80) covers entry — shared with Staunton, pays for itself in ~8 combined visits. No reservation needed for day use.
